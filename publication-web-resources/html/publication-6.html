Innovation

Room

The Innovation Room is both a

physical and conceptual space for

exploring the future of teaching and learning at UKZN.

It creates opportunities for academics and stakeholders to engage with emerging educational technologies, digital pedagogy, artificial intelligence, immersive learning,

gamification, Moodle/Learn enhancement, and practical teaching innovation.

The Innovation Room is not a passive display space. It is a space for demonstration, conversation, experimentation, and hands-on engagement. It invites academics to see how

digital tools and learning design approaches can be used

purposefully to support teaching, assessment, feedback,

engagement, and evidence-informed reflection.

One emerging direction is the AI Learning Futures Lab approach. This positions the Innovation Room as a practical space where academics can watch, try, and take away usable ideas, work-flows, templates, or examples.

Innovation is therefore not about technology for its own sake. It is about using tools, design

thinking, and evidence to create

better learning experiences.

The Digital Champions Network (DCN) is envisioned as a community of practice for academics, instructional and learning designers, academic leaders, and teaching and learning stakeholders committed to improving the student learning experience through purposeful digital innovation.

The DCN creates space for colleagues to share ideas, reflect on practice, discuss challenges, explore evidence, and learn from one another. It supports collaboration across Schools, Colleges, support units, and, where possible, regional partners.
