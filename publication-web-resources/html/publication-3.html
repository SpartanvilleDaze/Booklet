The Digital Curriculum Transformation (DCT)

project is one of ILD’s flagship initiatives

It emerged from the recognition that curriculum renewal requires more than technical support. It requires structured engagement, pedagogical grounding, capacity building, reflection, and

evidence-informed improvement.

DCT supports academics in rethinking how learning spaces are designed, experienced, and

improved. It connects digital learning with curriculum goals, student learning needs, assessment practice, accessibility, engagement, and evidence of impact.

Creating a shared starting point, building ownership, and identifying priorities for

The Design Sprint cycle has now moved from delivery into follow-up. The current focus is on supporting participating academics to finalise deliverables, consolidate evidence, and prepare for Symposiumpresenta-tions.

The 2026 ILD Symposium extends this work by foregrounding evidence-led design,

student voice, academic reflection, and regional collaboration. The Symposium is structured around DCT project showcases, an open call for abstracts, and portfolio awards for sustained practice and innovation.

DCT Portfolio Awards

The DCT Portfolio Awards recognise academics whose digital

curriculum transformation work is sustained, reflective, and

evidence-led.

A strong portfolio tells the story of a teaching and learning journey: what was done, why it was done, how it was implemented, what evidence was gathered, and what was learned.

Portfolios may include reflective narratives, module or course design artefacts, screenshots of digital content, student feedback,

engagement data, performance evidence, peer feedback, and reflections on lessons learned.

The awards shift the focus from participation alone to evidence of practice. They encourage ac-ademics to document innovation, foreground student feedback, reflect on impact, and connect their work to the Scholarship of Teaching and Learning.

In this way, the Portfolio Awards align directly with the 2026 Symposium theme. They help show how digital curriculum transformation is informed by evidence, shaped through reflection, and translated into meaningful practice.
